48 Million Golfers, $7 Billion in New Wealth, and One Market

By Samantha Gornstein | The Edmund Bogen Team at Douglas Elliman

If you own a home in a premier golf community like The Polo Club, you’re sitting on more than a beautiful property — you’re sitting on an asset that’s being fueled by three of the most powerful market forces in recent memory.

Let’s connect the dots.

The Golf Boom Is Real — and It’s Not Slowing Down

Golf in America isn’t just having a moment — it’s having a movement. According to the National Golf Foundation, participation has surged to an all-time high of 48 million Americans who played golf in some form in the past year. On-course rounds topped 530 million for the fourth consecutive year above the half-billion mark.

What’s driving it? A post-pandemic lifestyle shift that made people prioritize outdoor recreation, health, and community. Golf checks every one of those boxes. And unlike previous booms that skewed older, this wave is younger, more diverse, and more female than ever before.

For communities like The Polo Club — which offers two championship golf courses and a world-class club experience — this surge in demand translates directly into a deeper, more motivated buyer pool.

$7 Billion in New Wealth — and South Florida Is the Destination

The wealth migration into Florida isn’t just a trend — it’s a tidal wave. According to recent IRS and industry data, Florida gained over $7 billion in adjusted gross income from interstate migration in a single year, more than any other state in the nation.

Where is this wealth coming from? Primarily from high-tax states like New York, New Jersey, Connecticut, Illinois, and California — exactly the states where Polo Club has historically drawn its buyer base. These are affluent families and individuals seeking:

•  Zero state income tax — keeping more of what they earn
•  Year-round outdoor living — golf, tennis, pickleball, and pool every day
•  World-class club communities — social connection, dining, fitness, and belonging
•  Proximity to culture and commerce — Boca Raton and Palm Beach County deliver both

The Polo Club sits squarely in the path of this migration. With its Boca Raton address, championship golf, and full-service club lifestyle, it’s precisely the type of community that attracts this incoming wealth.

One Market: Where Golf Demand Meets Wealth Migration

Here’s where it all comes together. When you combine a record number of golfers with billions of dollars in wealth flowing into South Florida, you get unprecedented demand for luxury golf community homes.

And The Polo Club isn’t standing still. The community’s ongoing multimillion-dollar clubhouse renovation — including reimagined dining venues, new family amenities, and refreshed social spaces — signals to the market that this is a club investing in its future. That kind of forward momentum gives buyers confidence, and confidence drives premium pricing.

The Bottom Line

The convergence of 48 million golfers, $7 billion in wealth migration, and a club that’s actively reinvesting in its future creates a rare window of opportunity for Polo Club homeowners. Demand is strong, supply in premier golf communities is limited, and the macro trends are firmly on your side.

What This Means If You’re Thinking About Selling

If you’ve been considering selling your home at The Polo Club, the market dynamics have rarely been more favorable. Here’s why:

1.  Buyer demand is at record levels — More golfers means more people searching for golf community homes.
2.  Incoming wealth is well-qualified — These buyers are coming from high-income markets with substantial equity.
3.  The club’s renovation enhances value — A multimillion-dollar investment in amenities lifts the entire community.
4.  Supply is limited — Premier golf communities in Boca Raton aren’t being built anymore. Scarcity supports pricing.
