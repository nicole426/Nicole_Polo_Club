New York's Tax Crisis Is South Florida's Gain — And It's Just Getting Started

By Samantha Gornstein | The Edmund Bogen Team | February 2026

New York's wealthiest residents are leaving — and they're bringing their buying power straight to South Florida. What started as a pandemic-era trend has become a full-blown migration, and the numbers tell a striking story.

New York State's combined tax burden — including income taxes that can exceed 13% for top earners and property taxes that rank among the nation's highest — has pushed many affluent homeowners past their breaking point. The $10,000 cap on state and local tax (SALT) deductions, which has been in effect since 2018, continues to make high-tax states financially punishing for the wealthy.

"We're seeing buyers who are saving $200,000 to $500,000 annually just by changing their primary residence to Florida. When you combine that with no state income tax and lower property tax rates, the financial case is overwhelming."

Why Palm Beach County Is the Top Destination

While all of South Florida has benefited from the migration, Palm Beach County has emerged as the premier destination for high-net-worth New Yorkers. The county offers a rare combination: world-class amenities, top-rated schools, championship golf courses, and gated luxury communities — all without the urban density and congestion of Miami-Dade.

Communities like Seven Bridges in Delray Beach have become particularly attractive to relocating families. With homes ranging from $1.4M to $4.7M, resort-style amenities, and a location that provides easy access to both Palm Beach and Fort Lauderdale, it represents the kind of lifestyle upgrade that makes the move irresistible.

What's Driving Demand in 2026

Several converging factors are accelerating the trend this year:

Remote Work Is Permanent. The companies that initially allowed remote work during COVID have made it a permanent policy. Finance professionals, tech executives, and entrepreneurs no longer need a New York address to maintain their careers.

New York's Budget Crisis. New York State faces a growing budget deficit, and lawmakers have signaled that further tax increases may be necessary. For wealthy residents already on the fence, this is the final push.

Infrastructure Investment in South Florida. Billions of dollars in infrastructure investment — from Brightline rail expansion to new cultural institutions — have transformed South Florida from a vacation destination into a world-class place to live year-round.

Generational Wealth Transfer. Many buyers are thinking about legacy planning. Florida's favorable estate tax laws (no state estate tax) make it an ideal place to establish residency for long-term wealth preservation.

The Market Impact

The influx of well-capitalized buyers from the Northeast has fundamentally changed the South Florida luxury market. These buyers are often paying cash, moving quickly, and competing aggressively for the best properties. In communities like Seven Bridges, well-priced listings are receiving multiple offers within days of hitting the market.

Year-to-date, Seven Bridges has seen strong activity with 9 active listings and 3 closed sales already in early 2026 — a pace that signals continued demand in the luxury segment.

For homeowners in premium communities, this migration represents a significant opportunity. Property values in Palm Beach County's top-tier neighborhoods have shown remarkable resilience, even as other markets have softened. The combination of limited inventory and sustained demand from relocating buyers continues to support strong pricing.

What This Means for You

Whether you're considering a move from the Northeast, exploring investment opportunities, or thinking about listing your property in one of Palm Beach County's luxury communities, understanding these market dynamics is essential.

The tax migration from New York to South Florida isn't slowing down — it's accelerating. And for those positioned in the right communities, the opportunities are significant.
